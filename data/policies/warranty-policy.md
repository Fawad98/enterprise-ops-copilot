# Zava Warranty Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-WAR-001
**Effective date:** 2026-01-01
**Owner:** Customer Operations

## 1. Standard warranty by category

| Category | Warranty period | Covers |
|---|---|---|
| Electronics | 12 months | Manufacturing defects, hardware failure under normal use |
| Home | 6 months | Manufacturing defects |
| Toys | 90 days | Manufacturing defects |
| Apparel | 30 days | Seam and material defects |
| Grocery | Not applicable | — |

The warranty period starts on the delivery date.

## 2. What the warranty does not cover

- Accidental damage, drops, or liquid damage
- Normal wear and tear
- Damage from misuse or unauthorized repair
- Consumable parts (batteries beyond 90 days, filters)

## 3. Extended warranty (ZavaCare)

Customers may purchase **ZavaCare** at checkout for eligible Electronics, extending coverage to **36 months** and adding accidental damage protection with up to two claims per year. ZavaCare must be purchased within 30 days of the original order.

## 4. Making a warranty claim

To file a warranty claim:

1. Confirm the item is within its warranty period.
2. Create a support ticket describing the defect (see the ticketing process in supplier-escalation.md for internal routing).
3. The customer ships the item back using a Zava-provided prepaid label.
4. On confirmation of a covered defect, Zava issues a repair, replacement, or refund at Zava's discretion.

Warranty claims are distinct from returns. A return is a change-of-mind or condition issue within the return window; a warranty claim is a defect within the warranty period. An item can be outside the return window but still within warranty.
